Healthcare in Malaysia

Malaysia's healthcare system has grown steadily over the years. This visualization explores key data points and metrics that offer valuable insights into the country's healthcare landscape and how it has changed over the years.

Hospital Bed Distribution by State (2022)

The availability of hospital beds is crucial in ensuring that healthcare facilities can meet the demands of patients, especially during emergencies or disease outbreaks. Adequate bed capacity is essential for timely treatment, patient comfort, and reducing overcrowding in hospitals. It also plays a key role in improving overall healthcare outcomes by ensuring that patients receive the care they need when they need it.

Blood donations by State in Malaysia (2006 - 2024)

Blood donations are a vital component of any healthcare system. Regular donations help maintain a stable blood supply, which is essential for saving lives during accidents, natural disasters, and medical procedures. Encouraging voluntary blood donations within a country not only improves public health but also fosters a sense of community and preparedness for unforeseen medical needs.

Monthly Organ Donation Pledges by State (2009 - 2024)

By highlighting variations in pledges over time and across regions, it provides insights into the effectiveness of awareness campaigns and organ donation initiatives, helping to identify patterns and areas where further efforts may be needed to encourage pledges.
